Simple Differential Equations

Last edited: September 9, 2022 Here is the most simple Differential Equation one could imagine: \begin{equation} \dv{x}{t} = f(t,x) \end{equation} Or, perhaps, we have a second order differential equation which is the same thing but in the second degree: \begin{equation} \dv[2]{x}{t} = f\qty(t,x,\dv{x}{t}) \end{equation} Then in which case, we have that the first most simple type of differential equation to be as follows: \begin{equation} \dv{x}{t} = x(t) \end{equation} If we can solve this, we can generalize this to most of other First-Order Differential Equations.

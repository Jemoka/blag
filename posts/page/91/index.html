semantic prime

Last edited: August 8, 2022 In NSM, semantic primes are the most fundimental “lexical units” (so they can be words, or morphemes, etc. the size doesn’t matter) across languages. They are the “core of a universal mental lexicon”. There are… guidelines for identifying semantic primes A semantic prime has to be found in every(ish?) natural language A semantic prime has to be indefinable by other primes proof for the existence of semantic primes Proof: given if the Strong Lexicalization Hypothesis holds, semantic primes must exist.

power utility

Last edited: December 12, 2022 power utility, or isoelastic utility, is a financial econometric is a utility that results absolute, constant relative risk aversion. i.e.: you tell me how risk averse you are exogenously, I tell you how much utility some consumption is. constituents some relative risk coefficient \(\gamma \in (0,1)\), higher more risk averse consumption of some asset \(C\) requirements Utility \(U( C)\) is defined by: \begin{equation} U( C) = \frac{c^{1-\gamma}-1}{1-\gamma} \end{equation} additional information As you can see, the higher \(\gamma\), the lower utility some consumption brings.

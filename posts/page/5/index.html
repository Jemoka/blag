Last edited: February 2, 2023 eigenvalue is the scalar needed to scale the basis element of a one dimensional invariant subspace of a Linear Map to represent the behavior of the map: \begin{equation} Tv = \lambda v \end{equation} Note we require \(v \neq 0\) because otherwise all scalars count. eigenvector is a vector that forms the basis list of length 1 of that 1-D invariant subspace under \(T\). “operators own eigenvalues, eigenvalues own eigenvectors” Why is eigenvalue consistent per eigenvector?

map restriction operator

Last edited: February 2, 2023 Suppose \(T \in \mathcal{L}(V)\), and \(U \subset V\), an invariant subspace under \(T\). Then: \begin{equation} T|_{U}(u) = Tu,\ \forall u \in U \end{equation} where \(T|_{U} \in \mathcal{L}(U)\)

quotient operator

Last edited: February 2, 2023 Suppose \(T \in \mathcal{L}(V)\), and \(U \subset V\), an invariant subspace under \(T\). Then: \begin{equation} (T / U)(v+U) = Tv+U, \forall v \in V \end{equation} where \(T / U \in \mathcal{L}(V / U)\) quotient operator is well-defined Why is this not possible for any subspace of \(V\)? This is because we need \(T\) to preserve the exact structure of the subspace we are quotienting out by; otherwise our affine subset maybe squished to various unexpected places.

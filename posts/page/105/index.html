The Unreasonable Effectiveness of Mathematics in the Natural Sciences

Last edited: August 8, 2022 The Unreasonable Effectiveness of Mathematics in the Natural Sciences is an article by the famous mathematician Eugene Wigner. (Wigner 1990) Reflection What I found most peculiarly interesting is the focus on many mathematical/physics texts on the idea of the “beauty” of the expressions; and, it seems, the clear pleasure that Wigner gets from analyzing the systems with the aforementioned “beauty.” Setting aside whether or not this beauty is “deserved”/appropriate, I love that my attraction to physics is somewhat similar to what Wigner describes.

test for normality (statistics)

Last edited: April 4, 2022 Given what you claim as a normal distribution, we can test for its normality. Any distribution you claim as normal has to follow that: \begin{equation} np \geq 10 & n(1-p) \geq 10 \end{equation} that number of successes and failures need both be greater than or equal to ten.

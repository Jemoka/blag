Lagrangian Mechanics

Last edited: October 10, 2022 Want mechanics? No. You get energy. First, recall the stationary-action principle. To define a system in Lagrangian Mechanics, we define a smooth function \(L\), called the “Lagrangian”, and some configuration space (axis) \(M\). By convention, \(L=T-V\). \(T\) is the kinetic energy in the system, and \(V\) is the potential energy in the system. By the stationary-action principle, then, we require \(L\) to remain at a critical point (max, min, saddle.) This fact allows us to calculate the equations of motion by hold \(L\) at such a point, and evolving the \((T,V)\) pair to remain at that point.

asymtotic analysis

Last edited: September 9, 2022 ~ Given functions \(f(n)\) and \(g(n)\), if: \begin{equation} \lim_{n\to \infty} \left(\frac{f(n)}{g(n)}\right) = 1 \end{equation} we say that \(f \sim g\). That – the relationship between \(f\) and \(g\) grows in a similar fashion as \(n\) increases. For instance: \(f(n) = n+1\) \(g(n) = n+2\) Therefore: \begin{equation} f\sim g = \lim_{n\to \infty} \frac{f(n)}{g(n)} = \lim_{n\to \infty} \frac{n+1}{n+2} = 1 \end{equation} The \(\sim\) operator is commutative (\(f \sim g \Rightarrow g\sim f\)) and transitive (\(f\sim g, g\sim h \Rightarrow f \sim h\)).
